Things to Remember


  • Investing in the Right Equipment:

    Choose a camera and lens that can withstand underwater conditions. Consider factors like depth rating and ease of use.

  • Understanding Light and Composition

    Study how light behaves underwater. Use the rule of thirds for composition to create visually appealing images.

  • Patience and Respect for Marine Life:

    Avoid disturbing the natural habitat. Wait for the perfect moment when sea creatures are at ease.

  • Post-Processing Techniques:

    Learn to use software like Lightroom or Photoshop. Focus on enhancing colors that are often muted underwater.
